Supporting context · Why the underlying thesis holds

Hims is a platform business being priced as a failed GLP-1 trade

Before Hims touched weight loss, it was already a profitable business: sexual health, hair loss, dermatology, and mental health generated $1.2B+ of the $1.5B in 2024 revenue. GLP-1 compounding was an accelerant layered on top of a working platform — not the platform itself. The regulatory crackdown closed the compounding loophole but didn't touch the underlying business. 2.5M subscribers, $2.35B in 2025 revenue (+59%), and a $300–375M EBITDA guide for 2026 are the numbers of a company that isn't broken.

The Novo settlement is the pivot. Hims went from being sued and referred to the DOJ to being Novo's preferred direct-to-consumer distribution partner — the lowest price point for branded Wegovy in the US at $149/month. That's a real structural position in the world's largest pharmaceutical category, with zero legal risk attached. The Eucalyptus acquisition adds $450M ARR growing at triple digits in international markets with less regulatory friction, closing in weeks. The platform thesis is intact. The market hasn't fully caught up.

Verdict · Hidden Leaf Equity Research

Leg 2 is the better-structured trade. Leg 1 is a momentum bet with a tight window.

The thesis is sound and the recovery is real — but timing matters in short-dated options. Leg 1 (May 1) has no scheduled catalyst before expiry. It works if the recent rally continues, but there's nothing concrete to point to. Eight days of momentum with no earnings, no FDA meeting, no acquisition news. It's possible — the stock has been moving fast — but it's not a catalyst-driven setup.

Leg 2 (June 7) is the cleaner trade. May 11 earnings lands squarely between entry and expiry, and that print will contain the first real data on Novo conversion rates, subscriber retention, and Eucalyptus timing — the three core thesis drivers in a single event. A clean beat-and-raise on May 11 could push the stock above $37.11 on its own, leaving a month of additional time value. That's a genuine event-driven setup.

If Leg 1 moves into or near the money before May 1, consider taking the profit early rather than holding to expiration. Theta decay in the final week will erode value faster than the stock can likely move without a catalyst. Leg 2 has the earnings print as its foundation — hold it through May 11 before deciding.
